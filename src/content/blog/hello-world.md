## Why Build a Personal Site?

There's something deeply satisfying about carving out a small corner of the internet that's entirely your own. No algorithms deciding what gets seen, no character limits, no feed optimization — just a space to think, build, and share at your own pace.

I've been meaning to do this for a long time. Years of notes scattered across apps, ideas captured in half-finished documents, projects that never got a proper write-up. This site is my attempt to change that — to create a durable home for the things I care about.

### What You'll Find Here

This space will evolve over time, but here's the rough plan:

- **Writing** — Essays and technical posts on engineering, design, and whatever else catches my attention
- **Projects** — Documented work with context on the *why*, not just the *what*
- **Research** — Notes, explorations, and ideas-in-progress from things I'm studying
- **Reading** — Books, articles, and resources that have shaped my thinking

> "The best time to plant a tree was twenty years ago. The second best time is now."

### A Note on Building in Public

I believe there's real value in showing your work, even when it's messy. Not every post here will be polished. Some will be rough explorations, some will be wrong, and that's fine. The goal isn't perfection — it's progress.

Here's a small snippet that captures the spirit of how this site was bootstrapped:

```bash
npm create astro@latest
# chose: empty project, strict TypeScript, install dependencies
```

If you're reading this, welcome. I hope you find something useful, interesting, or at least mildly entertaining. And if you're thinking about building your own space on the web — just start. You can figure out the rest as you go.
